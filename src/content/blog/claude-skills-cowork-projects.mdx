*Part 1 of my Getting Started with Claude series.*

Claude has dominated recent months with continuous updates from Anthropic. Rather than chase every new feature, this post is about establishing foundational understanding. No prior Claude experience needed.

By the end, you'll know how to: use **Skills** (launched late 2025) and understand **Projects in Cowork** (launched March 2026). I'll use One Piece Season 2 as the demo subject — because why not.

## What Are Claude Skills

Claude can generate reports with clean structure, but specific use cases require customization. I wanted an 8-page One Piece digital zine; without skill guidance, Claude produced "a plain PDF that felt like a school report."

Skills are instruction sets (markdown files) loaded into Claude's context to give it new capabilities. Think of them less like plugins and more like a detailed recipe: here are the ingredients, here's the method.

Anthropic maintains public skills for Word documents, PDFs, spreadsheets, and PowerPoint files. For this demo I used two:
- Anthropic's `docx` skill for formatted Word documents
- A custom zine skill — styled HTML with specific layout, typography, One Piece's official color palette, and a page-reveal animation

The workflow chains both: the docx skill produces research, then the zine skill transforms that research into finished output.

## What Projects vs Cowork Projects Actually Are

Projects existed in claude.ai already. Claude later introduced **Projects for Cowork**. I find Cowork Projects most useful for local folder work; regular Projects suit ongoing tasks without local files.

Cowork Projects give Claude more context. Everything related to your goal — conversations, local files, instructions, skills — lives in one place. Claude knows the project's purpose without re-explanation each time you open it.

**Components of a Cowork Project:**

- **Instructions** — define project purpose and Claude's role (one short paragraph is optimal)
- **Skills** — workflows available in every project conversation
- **Memory** — facts Claude recalls across sessions
- **Folders** — all files on disk

> **Mental Model:** Folders are where your work lives → Projects are where you do the work → Skills are how Claude does it well.

## The Demo: One Piece Skill and Project Setup

Netflix's One Piece Season 2 launched recently. Creator Eiichiro Oda began the series in 1997 at age 22; it became the best-selling manga in history with over 500 million copies sold. Season 2 — "Into the Grand Line" — dropped March 10, 2026, trending #1 on Netflix.

I set up an "One Piece Content Hub" in Cowork with:
- Two folders: `research/` and `output/`
- Two loaded skills: Anthropic's docx skill + custom One Piece zine skill

**Project instructions defined a two-step workflow:**
1. Use docx skill to produce a research document on the provided topic, save to `research/` folder
2. Use zine skill to transform research into styled HTML zine, save to `output/` folder

### Prompt 1 — Research Document

```
Create a research document about One Piece; cover Eiichiro Oda as the author, 
the manga series and why it became the best-selling of all time, how Oda was 
personally involved in the Netflix live-action casting, why the adaptation worked, 
and a timeline of key milestones including Season 1 and Season 2.
```

Output: `research/one-piece-research.docx`

### Prompt 2 — Zine

```
Transform the research document in the research folder into an interactive One Piece zine.
```

Output: `output/onepiece-zine.html`

The zine opens with a cover page featuring One Piece title treatment in bold Bebas Neue typography with a red shadow, gold subtitle, a decorative sun circle, and a wave pattern — using official colors:

- Midnight: `#2E333F`
- Luffy red: `#D70000`
- Ocean blue: `#2E62A3`
- Sky blue: `#62C3F8`
- Gold: `#FFCD00`

All eight pages reveal sequentially. The HTML file opens in any browser, can be shared as a link, or printed.

## When This Setup Is Worth It (And When It Isn't)

Skills and Projects are worth setting up when:
- You're producing the **same output type repeatedly**
- **Format itself communicates meaning** (a zine vs a plain doc)

Skip the setup for plain text work — quick emails, decision drafting, brainstorming — where a fresh conversation suffices and the overhead outweighs the benefit.
